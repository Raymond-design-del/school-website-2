Our History

The idea of starting the school was born in 1995 through the inspiration of the current bishop of the Catholic Diocese of Homabay Bishop Michael Odiwa who was then the Parish Priest of Asumbi in collaboration with Francisan sisters of St. Joseph, Parish council and the Catholic Teachers Welfare Association.

The school took its name off a Mill Hill Missionary Priest of Dutch origin - Fr. Phillip Scheffer.

The school first opened its doors for admission on 12th January 1996 with a population of 88 learners occupying std 4 and 5. It is worth noting that at this particular moment the learners were being housed in the old renovated that was formally used by Catechumen. The building was basically used as classes and a staffroom while accomodation assistance was offered by Asumbi Teachers College for some time.

On 17th March, 1996, the school was officialy inaugurated by the late Bishop Linus Okok the Bishop of the Catholic Diocese of Homabay.

The school has a history of a journey of full treasured memories that saw the founder members and the Franciscan Sisters of St. Joseph having a desire to get more expansive land that occupies the present school.
